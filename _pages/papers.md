# Master thesis
**The economic geography effects of the Free Economic Zones: Evidence from Colombia** [Paper Results](https://www.dropbox.com/s/llt731gyqubg5rt/Master%20Thesis.pdf?dl=0) 

Governments in different types of economies have provided incentives to promote the economic development of their most remote and lagged areas, mitigating disparities in their regions. In particular, Special Economic Zones (SEZs) are considered one of the main place-based policy instruments for economic development and growth, by enhancing industry competitiveness and attracting foreign direct investment. The zone development has grown exponentially over the last few year, mainly concentrated in developing economies, which have adopted it as an instrument of economic development attempting to mitigate the regional disparities by promoting economic development in the most lagged areas. Although it is an ambitious regime, there is still a little literature studying the policy evaluation effectiveness of these special economic zones. In this paper, I exploit the institutional framework of the free economic zones and their importance for the Colombian economy. The Colombian legislation has specified the goals that must be accomplished by the regime, the most important relies on the promotion and generation of employment. Therefore, I evaluate the impact of the free economic zone policy, or in Spanish "Zona Franca", on the population displacement in Colombia from 1993 to 2018. Furthermore, I consider a theoretical framework to understand the mechanism behind the free economic zone, explaining that the tax incentives are an efficient tool to attract firms to specific locations through a differential in marginal cost.
